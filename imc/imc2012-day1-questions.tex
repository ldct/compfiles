\section*{Problem 1}
For every positive integer $n$, let $p(n)$ denote the number of ways to express $n$ as a sum of positive integers. (For instance, $p(4) = 5$.) Also define $p(0) = 1$. Prove that $p(n) - p(n-1)$ is the number of ways to express $n$ as a sum of integers each strictly greater than $1$.

\section*{Problem 2}
Let $n$ be a fixed positive integer. Determine the smallest possible rank of an $n \times n$ matrix that has zeros along the main diagonal and strictly positive real numbers off the main diagonal.

\section*{Problem 3}
Given an integer $n > 1$, let $S_n$ be the group of permutations of $\{1, 2, \dots, n\}$. Two players, $A$ and $B$, take turns selecting distinct elements of $S_n$. The game ends when the selected elements generate $S_n$; the player making the last move loses. $A$ moves first. Which player has a winning strategy?

\section*{Problem 4}
Let $f : \mathbb{R} \to \mathbb{R}$ be a continuously differentiable function satisfying $f'(t) > f(f(t))$ for all $t \in \mathbb{R}$. Prove that $f(f(f(t))) \le 0$ for all $t \ge 0$.

\section*{Problem 5}
Let $a$ be a rational number and $n$ a positive integer. Prove that the polynomial $X^{2^n}(X+a)^{2^n} + 1$ is irreducible in $\mathbb{Q}[X]$.

\section*{Problem 6}
Consider a polynomial $f(x) = x^{2012} + a_{2011} x^{2011} + \dots + a_1 x + a_0$. Albert Einstein and Homer Simpson take turns assigning real values to the coefficients (Albert first, each coefficient once). Homer wants $m(x) \mid f(x)$, Albert wants to prevent this.

(a) $m(x) = x - 2012$. (b) $m(x) = x^2 + 1$. Who has a winning strategy?

\section*{Problem 7}
Define $a_0 = 1$, $a_1 = 1/2$, and $a_{n+1} = \frac{n a_n^2}{1 + (n+1) a_n}$ for $n \ge 1$. Show that $\sum_{k=0}^\infty \frac{a_{k+1}}{a_k}$ converges and find its value.

\section*{Problem 8}
Is the set of positive integers $n$ such that $n! + 1$ divides $(2012 n)!$ finite or infinite?

\section*{Problem 9}
Let $n \ge 2$ be an integer. Find all real numbers $a$ such that there exist real numbers $x_1, \dots, x_n$ satisfying
\[ x_1(1-x_2) = x_2(1-x_3) = \dots = x_{n-1}(1-x_n) = x_n(1-x_1) = a. \]

\section*{Problem 10}
Let $c \ge 1$ be a real number. Let $G$ be an abelian group and $A \subset G$ a finite set with $|A+A| \le c|A|$. Prove that $|\underbrace{A + A + \dots + A}_{k}| \le c^k |A|$ for every positive integer $k$.

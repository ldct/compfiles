\section*{Problem 1}
Evaluate the product
\[ \prod_{n=3}^\infty \frac{(n^3 + 3n)^2}{n^6 - 64}. \]

\section*{Problem 2}
A four-digit number $YEAR$ is called very good if the system
\begin{align*}
Yx + Ey + Az + Rw &= Y \\
Rx + Yy + Ez + Aw &= E \\
Ax + Ry + Yz + Ew &= A \\
Ex + Ay + Rz + Yw &= R
\end{align*}
has at least two solutions. Find all very good years in the 21st century.

\section*{Problem 3}
Let $f : (-1, 1) \to \mathbb{R}$ be twice differentiable with $2f'(x) + x f''(x) \geq 1$ for $x \in (-1, 1)$. Prove that $\int_{-1}^1 x f(x)\, dx \geq \tfrac{1}{3}$.

\section*{Problem 4}
Define $a_0 = 1$, $a_1 = 2$, $(n+3) a_{n+2} = (6n+9) a_{n+1} - n a_n$. Prove that all terms are integers.

\section*{Problem 5}
Determine whether there exist odd $n$ and integer $n \times n$ matrices $A, B$ with $\det(B) = 1$, $AB = BA$, and $A^4 + 4A^2 B^2 + 16 B^4 = 2019 I$.

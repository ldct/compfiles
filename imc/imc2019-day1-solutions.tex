\section*{Problem 1}
\subsection*{Solution}
Answer: $72/7$.

\[ a_n = \frac{n^2(n^2+3)^2}{(n-2)(n^2+2n+4)(n+2)(n^2-2n+4)} = \frac{n}{n-2} \cdot \frac{n}{n+2} \cdot \frac{n^2+3}{(n-1)^2+3} \cdot \frac{n^2+3}{(n+1)^2+3}. \]
Telescoping from $n = 3$ to $N$:
\[ \prod_{n=3}^N a_n = \frac{N(N-1)}{1 \cdot 2} \cdot \frac{3 \cdot 4}{(N+1)(N+2)} \cdot \frac{N^2+3}{7} \cdot \frac{12}{(N+1)^2+3} \to \frac{72}{7}. \]

\section*{Problem 2}
\subsection*{Solution}
The system has multiple solutions iff the coefficient matrix is singular. Row reducing (sum of all rows $= Y+E+A+R$), the matrix is singular iff one of:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $E \neq R$ and $A + Y = E + R$
\item $E = R$ and $Y = A$
\item $E = R$, $A \neq Y$ and $Y + A = 2R$
\end{enumerate}
For $Y = 2, E = 0$: case 1 gives $A + 2 = R$ with $R \neq 0$: years 2002, 2013, 2024, 2035, 2046, 2057, 2068, 2079. Case 2 gives 2020. Case 3 is impossible. Total: 9 years.

\section*{Problem 3}
\subsection*{Solution}
Let $g(x) = x f(x) - x^2/2$. Then $g'(x) = f(x) + x f'(x) - x$, and $g''(x) = 2 f'(x) + x f''(x) - 1 \geq 0$, so $g$ is convex. With $g(0) = 0$, the tangent line at $0$ is $g'(0) x = ax$, and by convexity $g(x) \geq ax$. Thus
\[ \int_{-1}^1 x f(x)\, dx = \int_{-1}^1 \left(g(x) + \tfrac{x^2}{2}\right) dx \geq \int_{-1}^1 \left(ax + \tfrac{x^2}{2}\right) dx = \tfrac{1}{3}. \]

\section*{Problem 4}
\subsection*{Solution}
Let $f(x) = \sum a_n x^n$. The recurrence yields (after manipulation) the ODE $(x^3 - 6x^2 + x) f'(x) + (1 - 3x) f(x) - 1 - x = 0$. Solving with $f(0) = 1$:
\[ f(x) = \frac{1 - x - \sqrt{x^2 - 6x + 1}}{2x}. \]
Then $f$ satisfies $x f(x)^2 - (1-x) f(x) + 1 = 0$. Expanding gives
\[ \sum_{n \geq 0} \left(\sum_{k=0}^n a_k a_{n-k}\right) x^{n+1} = \sum_{n \geq 0} (a_{n+1} - a_n) x^{n+1}, \]
so $a_{n+1} = a_n + \sum_{k=0}^n a_k a_{n-k}$. By induction from $a_0 = 1, a_1 = 2 \in \mathbb{Z}$, all $a_n \in \mathbb{Z}$.

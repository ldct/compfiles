\section*{Problem 1}
Let $A$ and $B$ be real symmetric matrices with all eigenvalues strictly greater than $1$. Let $\lambda$ be a real eigenvalue of matrix $AB$. Prove that $|\lambda| > 1$.

\section*{Problem 2}
Let $f : \mathbb{R} \to \mathbb{R}$ be a twice differentiable function. Suppose $f(0) = 0$. Prove that there exists $\xi \in (-\pi/2, \pi/2)$ such that
\[ f''(\xi) = f(\xi)(1 + 2\tan^2 \xi). \]

\section*{Problem 3}
There are $2n$ students in a school ($n \ge 2$). Each week $n$ students go on a trip. After several trips, every two students were together on at least one trip. What is the minimum number of trips needed?

\section*{Problem 4}
Let $n \ge 3$ and let $x_1, \dots, x_n$ be nonnegative reals. Define $A = \sum x_i$, $B = \sum x_i^2$, $C = \sum x_i^3$. Prove that
\[ (n+1) A^2 B + (n-2) B^2 \ge A^4 + (2n-2) AC. \]

\section*{Problem 5}
Does there exist a sequence $(a_n)$ of complex numbers such that for every positive integer $p$, $\sum_{n=1}^\infty a_n^p$ converges if and only if $p$ is not a prime?
